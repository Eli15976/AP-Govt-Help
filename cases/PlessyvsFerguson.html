Plessy vs Ferguson (1896)

back home

Issue

Louisiana had a separation between colored and whites while riding a car. However, plessy, who is 1/8 colored, sat in a white's car and refused to move when he was asked to. He believes this is violating his equal protection, so he sued.

Parts of the Constitution Involved

The 14th amendment was involved in this case, because Plessy thinks that he wasn't equally protected.

Decision

The court was in favor of Ferguson (7-2). According to them, the 14th amendment wasn't violated because even thought it is separate, it is still equal. They see no change in treatment of the 2 cars.
