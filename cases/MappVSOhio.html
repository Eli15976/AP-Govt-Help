AP US Government

Mapp vs Ohio

back home

Issue

Police searched Mapp's house without a warrent because the police believed that she was protecting a suspected bomber. Though they did not find any evidence, Mapp was arrested for possessing obscene pictures. Mapp sued because the Police did NOT have a warrent to search her house.

Parts of the Constitution Involved

The 4th amendment was involved in this case. People are allowed to be secure in their own homes, and not unlawful or unreasonable seizures should or will be allowed.

Decision

The court was in favor of Mapp (5-4). Evidence without a warrent would be nullified or void. You cannot use evidence that has been obtained illegally.
