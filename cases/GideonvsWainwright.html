Gideon vs Wainwright (1963)

back home

Issue

Gideon broke into a pool hall and stole money. That was indeed a crime that he did. However, during his trial in, he requested for a lawyer and did not get one. The state government said that lawyers were reserved for the poor and major crimes that would deserve a death punishment if the defender loses. Gideon sued because he thought is was unfair for him since he doesn't have someone as knowledgable to help defend him.

Parts of the Constitution Involved

The 6th and 14th amendment were involved in this case. 6th amendment, to have a right to a lawyer when requested. 14th amendment, states cannot take away the rights to provide a lawyer.

Decision

The court was in favor of Gideon (9-0). Courts should provide a lawyer when the defendant asks for one. Without a lawyer, the defendant cannot properly defend itself from the accuser.
